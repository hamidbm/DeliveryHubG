# Administration and Governance

This capability is played by the **Admin** module.

## What It Means

Admin is the governance and configuration backbone of DeliveryHub.

It is where the organization defines the shared rules and reference data that make the rest of the product consistent and trustworthy.

## What Is Implemented

### Reference data and content setup

Admins can manage:

- bundles
- applications
- categories and document types
- wiki themes
- wiki templates
- diagram templates

### Ownership and accountability mapping

Bundle assignments define ownership relationships that influence:

- visibility
- governance accountability
- delivery context
- review routing

### AI governance

Admins can configure provider routing, defaults, controls, and runtime AI behavior so AI remains managed rather than ad hoc.

### Delivery policy

The product supports configurable governance thresholds and policy settings, including bundle-level overrides where needed.

### Sample data and bootstrap support

Admins can install or reset curated sample content for demos and testing.

### Audit surfaces

Admin includes event and notification audit views so the organization can inspect what happened and why.

### Integrations

The module includes integration support for external systems such as Jira and GitHub.

### Backup and restore

Configuration and planning metadata can be exported and imported in a controlled way.

## Why These Features Matter

- consistent governance rules across the program
- clearer ownership and accountability
- safer AI usage
- easier demo and environment setup
- better operational and audit visibility

## Who Gets the Most Value

### Admins and governance leads

Use Admin to keep the platform coherent and aligned with policy.

### PMO and senior leadership

Use Admin-driven outputs such as ownership and policy to trust what the rest of the product is showing.

## How Executives Should Use This in Practice

### CIO

Use Admin-governed ownership, policy, and AI controls to ensure the platform reflects organizational rules rather than informal team behavior.

### VP

Use governance settings and audit views to understand whether escalation paths, ownership mapping, and decision controls are being applied consistently.

### PMO

Use policy, sample data controls, and audit surfaces to keep environments reliable and reporting trustworthy.

### Architects

Use template, taxonomy, and assignment governance so architecture and knowledge content stay standardized across the program.

### Delivery managers

Rely on the governance settings indirectly by trusting that ownership, review routing, and planning thresholds are consistently configured.
