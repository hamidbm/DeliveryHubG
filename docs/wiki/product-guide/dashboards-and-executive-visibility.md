# Dashboards and Executive Visibility

This capability is played by the **Dashboards** module.

## What It Means

Dashboards turn DeliveryHub from an operational system into a management system.

They help leaders answer:

- are we on track?
- where are the biggest risks?
- which bundles or milestones need intervention?
- are things getting better or worse?

## What Is Implemented

### Executive dashboard

DeliveryHub includes an executive-oriented dashboard that aggregates data across bundles, applications, milestones, and work items.

### Bundle and milestone dashboards

Leaders and managers can move from the portfolio level into more focused bundle or milestone views.

### Program health and delivery rollups

Dashboards surface:

- progress
- risk
- overdue work
- blocked work
- confidence and readiness

### Program capacity visibility

The system can compare committed demand against available bundle capacity so overload becomes visible before it causes downstream slippage.

### Executive Decision Dashboard

The dashboard redesign moves the experience beyond static counts and toward a decision-oriented view of:

- delivery health
- forecast direction
- intervention priorities
- risk concentration

### Delivery progress and forecast views

Dashboards visualize planned progress versus actual progress and highlight likely finish movement before missed dates become official.

### Blocker heatmaps and risk trends

Leadership can see where blockers and risks are accumulating, whether they are spreading, and whether the situation is improving or deteriorating.

### Velocity, capacity, and execution mechanics

The dashboard layer is not limited to outcomes. It also exposes the operating mechanics behind those outcomes, such as velocity patterns, capacity utilization, and execution drag.

### Dependency risk mapping

DeliveryHub can surface structural risk created by dependencies so leaders can spot where one workstream may be destabilizing another.

### Health pulse and composition views

The dashboard can summarize portfolio composition and health distribution to show how much of the program is healthy, under watch, or at risk.

### AI summary panel

Important AI-generated signals are surfaced directly in the dashboard experience so leadership can move from observation to investigation quickly.

## Why These Features Matter

- senior stakeholders get a useful control surface instead of a decorative status page
- bundle and milestone drill-down is easier
- the product supports intervention, not only observation
- program discussions can be driven by the same facts everyone sees

## Who Gets the Most Value

### Upper management

Use Dashboards to get an executive summary of health, risk, and progress.

### PMO and program leadership

Use them to identify where to escalate, where to investigate, and where to allocate attention.

## How Executives Should Use This in Practice

### CIO

Start with the executive dashboard to identify the few bundles, milestones, or systemic risks that justify leadership intervention that week.

### VP

Use drill-down from portfolio to bundle views to separate broad concern from localized issues before escalating teams.

### PMO

Use dashboards in weekly operating reviews to anchor discussion on actual progress, blockers, forecast movement, and capacity pressure.

### Architects

Use dashboard signals to spot where dependency patterns, blocked reviews, or structural risks are affecting delivery health.

### Delivery managers

Use bundle and milestone dashboard views to prepare status updates, explain forecast movement, and identify the most urgent blockers.
