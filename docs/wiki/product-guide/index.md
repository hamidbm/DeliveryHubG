# Product Guide

This section explains DeliveryHub in business language for all audiences, especially delivery leaders, architects, PMO teams, and upper management.

## DeliveryHub at a Glance

DeliveryHub contains **four major apps** and **two major cross-cutting modules**.

## The Four Big Apps

### 1. Application Portfolio Management

Played by the **Applications** module.

This is the portfolio and APM layer of the product. It helps teams understand what systems exist, how they are grouped into bundles, how they depend on each other, and what delivery impact those systems create.

### 2. Jira-like Task Management

Played by the **Work Items** module.

This is the execution engine. It manages epics, features, stories, tasks, risks, dependencies, milestones, backlog flow, roadmaps, forecasting, and delivery planning.

### 3. LeanIX-like Architecture Functionality

Played by the **Architecture** module.

This is where teams manage architecture diagrams, integration views, design artifacts, and architecture review workflows.

### 4. Confluence-like Knowledge Functionality

Played by the **Knowledge** capability, powered by the **Wiki** module.

This is where teams create and organize knowledge pages, upload supporting files, collaborate on documentation, and launch reviews from documents.

## The Two Other Big Modules

### Dashboards

Dashboards are the portfolio and executive visibility layer. They help leadership understand overall health, risk concentration, progress, confidence, and where intervention is needed.

### AI Insights

AI Insights is the assistive decision-support layer. It turns structured portfolio data into summaries, trends, alerts, investigations, strategic questions, what-if scenarios, and recommended actions.
